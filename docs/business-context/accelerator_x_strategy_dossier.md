# Accelerator X: Vision and strategy discovery dossier

*Version 1.0 — February 2026*
*Status: Exploratory draft — divergent thinking captured, convergence pending*

---

## Executive summary

This document captures the strategic exploration undertaken to define Accelerator X's core model, offer architecture, and path to £10M+ revenue. It represents divergent thinking across multiple options, with preliminary recommendations and open questions for further refinement.

**Key conclusions so far:**

1. **Target customer is defined by mindset, not demographics.** We're targeting senior decision-makers who know AI matters, are ready to invest in real change, and want long-term partnership—regardless of industry.

2. **The business operates across three layers:** scalable enablement (digital products, events, content), premium strategic access (Andy and Toby's time), and outsourced implementation (CTO-led delivery team). Revenue at scale requires all three.

3. **The workshop is the strategic entry point.** It filters for serious buyers, delivers immediate value, generates strong revenue on its own, and converts at high rates to ongoing work.

4. **£10M revenue is achievable but requires deliberate choices.** The current hands-on model cannot scale to £10M. The path requires systematisation, a strong implementation layer, and potentially selective senior hires—but only if they unlock non-linear growth.

5. **The positioning is "anti-agency, anti-consultancy."** We build capability, not dependency. We deliver results, not slides. We stay until the job is done.

## Part 1: Who we're for

### The targeting approach

We rejected demographic targeting (e.g., "mid-market manufacturing companies, £20-50M revenue") in favour of **psychographic and behavioural targeting**. Our filter is a type of leader facing a type of moment, not a type of company.

### The ideal client profile

**Mindset and readiness:**

- Knows AI matters and cannot be ignored
- Wants fundamental, lasting change—not quick fixes
- Willing to invest properly (time, money, commitment)
- Currently overwhelmed and frozen, but ready to move

**Authority and access:**

- Senior decision-maker: CEO, founder, MD, C-suite, director level
- Can say yes without endless procurement or committee approval
- Values direct relationships over formal vendor processes

**Disqualifiers (who we don't work with):**

- Shortcut-seekers looking for magic bullets
- "Build me a chatbot and disappear" buyers
- Tyre-kickers who want free strategy
- Anyone who thinks AI transformation happens in weeks
- Price-shoppers who want the cheapest option

### Implications for go-to-market

This targeting approach means we cannot "buy a list" of prospects. Instead, our go-to-market must be built around:

1. **Content that attracts the right mindset** — thought leadership that resonates with leaders who are frozen but ready
2. **Network effects** — Toby's consulting network, Andy's tech and product network, referrals from satisfied clients
3. **The workshop as filter** — pricing (£4k+) and format (full day, serious commitment) naturally selects for serious buyers
4. **Authority positioning** — newsletter, YouTube, podcast, events that establish credibility and attract inbound

## Part 2: The three-layer business model

Through our exploration, a clear three-layer architecture emerged. Each layer serves a different purpose, has different economics, and scales differently.

### Layer 1: Enablement and activation (highly scalable)

**What it includes:**

- Online courses, frameworks, tools, templates
- Cohort-based programmes (not 1:1)
- Community membership
- High-ticket events (leadership "away days")
- Newsletter, YouTube, podcast, content engine

**Economics:**

- Low marginal cost per additional customer
- Revenue potential: £1-2M/year once built
- Requires upfront investment in content and systems
- Feeds the pipeline for Layers 2 and 3

**Why it matters:**
This is where 80-90% of the value we deliver could live—and it doesn't require us in the room. Basic AI literacy, tool proficiency, workflow fundamentals... these can be taught at scale. This layer is essential for reaching £10M without burning out.

**Current status:** Mostly planned, not yet built. Newsletter and content engine are priorities. Events concept is defined but not yet executed.

---

### Layer 2: Strategic access (premium, constrained)

**What it includes:**

- Direct advisory and coaching with Andy and Toby
- The 2-3 hours/week per client layer
- Strategic thinking, problem-solving, sounding board
- Reserved for clients who've done workshops or come in at high-ticket

**Economics:**

- High value per hour, but hours are finite
- Combined capacity: ~50 hours/week (25 each)
- At 2 hours/client = 25 clients max simultaneously
- Revenue ceiling: ~£2-3M/year from this layer alone

**Why it matters:**
This is the scarce, premium offering. It's what clients can't get from digital products or junior consultants. It's also what differentiates us from agencies and productised competitors. But it cannot scale beyond our personal capacity—so it must be priced accordingly and reserved for the right clients.

**Current status:** This is what we're doing now. Too hands-on (5-6 hours/week per client). Needs to be compressed to 2-3 hours without losing value.

---

### Layer 3: Implementation (outsourced, margin business)

**What it includes:**

- n8n builds, custom AI development, integrations
- Claude skills, knowledge bases, automation systems
- Technical architecture and infrastructure
- Delivered by CTO + trusted contractors/specialists

**Economics:**

- We scope, price, and oversee; they execute
- Target: 30-50% margin on implementation work
- Scales through other people's hands, not ours
- Revenue potential: £2-3M+ at scale with strong CTO layer

**Why it matters:**
Many clients need things built, not just advice. If we don't offer implementation, they'll go elsewhere—and we'll lose the relationship. But we can't do the building ourselves at scale. This layer lets us capture implementation revenue without consuming our time.

**Current status:** Ad hoc. No dedicated CTO yet. Exploring candidates. Model not yet systematised.

## Part 3: The offer architecture

### Entry point: The workshop

**Format:** One full day with client's leadership team
**Price:** £4,000-8,000 (likely increasing as demand grows)
**Guarantee:** Full money-back if no value delivered

**What happens:**

- Deep discovery of business, blockers, opportunities
- Hands-on exercises and AI exposure
- Clear roadmap and prioritised action plan
- Proposal for Phase 1 sprint if appropriate

**Strategic purpose:**

- Filters for serious buyers (willingness to pay upfront)
- Delivers immediate, tangible value
- Creates the proposal for ongoing work (paid discovery)
- High conversion to cycles (~70% expected)

**Capacity (Andy + Toby only):**

- 4 workshops/week maximum
- 192 workshops/year
- £768k-1.54M annual revenue from workshops alone

---

### Core engagement: 8-week cycles

**Format:** Focused sprint with defined scope and outcomes
**Price:** £20,000-25,000 per cycle
**Commitment:** ~2-3 hours/week of Andy or Toby's time (target state)

**What happens:**

- Weekly or fortnightly sessions (training, advisory, review)
- Async support and guidance
- Hands-on implementation support (via Layer 3)
- Clear deliverables and measurable outcomes each cycle

**Value commitment:**

- Each cycle should deliver 10x+ ROI
- £25k investment → £250k+ measurable value in year one
- Value drivers: cost savings, efficiency gains, revenue growth, new capabilities

**Retention model:**

- Clients run as many cycles as needed
- Average client: 2-3 cycles expected
- Some clients: ongoing retained relationship

---

### High-ticket events

**Format:** Exclusive leadership "away days" (weekend or 2-day intensive)
**Price:** £10,000 per participant
**Frequency:** 2-3 events per year
**Capacity:** 20-40 participants per event

**What happens:**

- Immersive AI transformation experience
- Peer network of like-minded leaders
- Direct access to Andy and Toby
- Actionable frameworks and breakthroughs

**Revenue potential:** £600k-1.2M/year

**Strategic purpose:**

- High-margin, concentrated delivery
- Network and community building
- Authority and brand positioning
- Pipeline for ongoing work

**Current status:** Concept defined, not yet executed.

---

### Digital products and content (Layer 1)

**Planned components:**

- Online courses (101-level AI literacy, tool proficiency)
- Frameworks, templates, tools (immediately applicable)
- Community membership (ongoing access, peer support)
- Newsletter (authority building, pipeline nurturing)
- YouTube and podcast (reach, credibility, inbound)

**Revenue model:**

- Low-ticket self-serve products (£100-500)
- Mid-ticket courses (£1,000-3,000)
- Community membership (£50-200/month)
- Free content → paid funnel

**Strategic purpose:**

- Scalable revenue not dependent on our time
- Feeds pipeline for workshops and cycles
- Establishes authority and reach
- Enables "AI literacy at scale" without us in the room

**Current status:** Mostly planned. Need to prioritise and build.

---

### Implementation services (Layer 3)

**What we offer:**

- Custom AI development (full-stack web/mobile apps)
- Knowledge bases and AI agents
- n8n automation development
- Claude skills and system architecture
- Integration and technical implementation

**Delivery model:**

- Andy and Toby scope and oversee
- CTO leads technical delivery
- Contractor bench executes

**Economics:**

- Price to client: project-based, varies by scope
- Margin: 30-50% after contractor costs
- No upper limit on volume (scales with contractor capacity)

**Current status:** Delivered ad hoc. CTO candidate in discussion. Model not yet systematised.

---

**Fractional advisory:**

- Retained relationships (monthly fee for ongoing access)
- Board advisory roles
- Could be significant recurring revenue

**JV and equity arrangements:**

- Co-developing assets with clients

- Already in discussion with one client

- Creates recurring, high-margin revenue

## Part 4: The path to £10M

### The maths at current state

**If we only did what we're doing now (workshops + cycles, just Andy and Toby):**

- 192 workshops/year × £6k average = £1.15M
- 70% convert to cycles = 134 clients
- Average 2 cycles each × £22.5k = £6M from cycles
- **Total: ~£7M**

**But the constraint:**
134 clients in cycles. Even at 2 hours/week, that's 268 hours/week. Andy and Toby have ~50 combined. The model physically cannot deliver.

**Realistic delivery capacity (Layer 2 only):**

- 25 clients simultaneously at 2 hours each
- ~75 cycles/year (clients turning over)
- 75 × £22.5k = £1.7M from cycles
- Plus workshops £1.15M
- **Total: ~£2.9M ceiling with just Andy and Toby delivering**

---

### The levers to reach £10M

| Lever | Revenue contribution | How it works |
|-------|---------------------|--------------|
| **Workshops** | £1-1.5M | Andy and Toby deliver 4/week |
| **Cycles (Layer 2)** | £1.5-2M | 25 clients, ~2 hours each, premium pricing |
| **Events** | £0.6-1.2M | 2-3 events, 20-40 people, £10k tickets |
| **Digital products (Layer 1)** | £0.5-1.5M | Courses, community, content monetisation |
| **Implementation (Layer 3)** | £2-4M | CTO + contractors, 40% margin on £5-10M billings |
| **Total potential** | **£6-10M** | |

---

### What has to be true for £10M

1. **Layer 1 must be built and generating £1M+.** Digital products, community, events need to be live and scaled.

2. **Layer 3 must be substantial.** A strong CTO with a reliable contractor bench, delivering £5-10M in implementation work at 30-50% margin.

3. **Workshop conversion stays high.** 60-70% conversion to cycles; this is the engine that feeds everything.

4. **Cycle delivery is systematised.** 2-3 hours/week per client maximum. AI-assisted delivery, clear frameworks, minimal waste.

5. **Pricing holds or increases.** Workshops at £6-8k, cycles at £25k+, events at £10k. No discounting.

6. **We don't add headcount unnecessarily.** Every hire must unlock non-linear growth. No linear scaling.

## Part 5: Options for growth

### Option A: Stay at two, maximise leverage

**Model:** Andy and Toby only. No senior hires. Maximum systematisation and AI leverage.

**Revenue ceiling:** £3-4M (workshops, cycles, events, some digital products)

**Pros:**

- Simplest to manage
- Highest margin percentage
- No partner complexity
- Full control

**Cons:**

- Limited by personal capacity
- Can't capture full implementation opportunity
- Risk if either person is unavailable
- Slower to build Layer 1

**Verdict:** Works if the goal is lifestyle business or stepping stone. Doesn't reach £10M.

---

### Option B: Add CTO, build implementation layer

**Model:** Andy and Toby for strategy and advisory. CTO leads technical delivery with contractor bench.

**Revenue potential:** £5-7M (existing layers + £2-3M implementation margin)

**Pros:**

- Captures implementation revenue
- Technical credibility
- Frees Andy from hands-on build work
- Scalable through contractors

**Cons:**

- CTO equity/compensation complexity
- Quality control on contractor work
- Management overhead
- Risk if CTO doesn't work out

**Verdict:** Strong option. Probably necessary to reach £7M+. CTO candidate in discussion.

---

### Option C: Add senior advisors/partners

**Model:** 2-3 additional senior people who can deliver at Toby/Andy level.

**Revenue potential:** £7-10M (multiplied Layer 2 capacity + everything else)

**Pros:**

- Expands delivery capacity for premium work
- May bring new networks and deal flow
- Risk distribution
- Succession path

**Cons:**

- Only works if they bring non-linear value (network, not just hours)
- Equity dilution and governance complexity
- Risk of quality inconsistency
- Cultural alignment challenges

**Conditions for this to work:**

- Each new partner must bring pipeline we can't otherwise access
- Or free Andy/Toby for higher-leverage activities
- Or open new verticals/geographies

**Verdict:** Potentially valuable, but only with the right people. Do not hire for capacity; hire for capability and network.

---

### Option D: Integrated model (recommended direction)

**Model:** Combination of B and selective elements of C, with strong Layer 1.

**Team:**

- Andy and Toby (co-founders, 50-50)
- CTO (equity partner or senior hire, leads Layer 3)
- Potentially 1-2 additional senior advisors (only if they unlock non-linear growth)
- Contractor bench for implementation

**Revenue architecture:**

- Layer 1 (digital, events): £1.5-2M
- Layer 2 (Andy, Toby, select partners): £2-3M
- Layer 3 (CTO + contractors): £3-4M
- **Total: £7-9M**, with path to £10M+

**Pros:**

- Balanced growth across all layers
- Captures implementation opportunity
- Preserves premium positioning
- Scalable without excessive headcount

**Cons:**

- More moving parts
- Requires strong CTO hire
- Partner selection critical

**Verdict:** Most likely path to £10M while preserving the lean, high-margin model.

## Part 6: Open questions and risks

### Strategic questions still to resolve

1. **CTO candidate:** Is the current candidate the right fit? What's the equity/compensation structure?

2. **Layer 1 priority:** Which digital products do we build first? Newsletter seems obvious, but what's the MVP course or community?

3. **Events:** Do we pilot one event in 2025, or wait until 2026?

4. **Recruitment angle:** Do we integrate Talent Aisle, keep it separate, or table this entirely?

5. **Partner criteria:** What exactly makes someone worth adding? How do we evaluate "non-linear value"?

6. **Pricing strategy:** Do we push workshop pricing to £8k now, or wait for more demand?

---

### Key risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Workshop conversion lower than 70% | Medium | High | Test and refine, track closely |
| CTO hire doesn't work out | Medium | High | Clear trial period, equity vesting |
| Layer 1 takes too long to build | High | Medium | Prioritise ruthlessly, start with newsletter |
| Implementation quality issues | Medium | High | Strong CTO, clear QA process |
| Pricing resistance | Low | Medium | Hold the line, target right clients |
| Capacity crunch before systems ready | High | Medium | Say no to work, protect margin |

We've proven that two founders plus smart leverage can build something substantial—and we know exactly what's required to double again.
