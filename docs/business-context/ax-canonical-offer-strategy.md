## 5) Canonical offer ladder

The offer ladder has three operational layers and one clear client progression path.

### Layer 1 — Scalable enablement (low founder dependency)

Includes:

- Free authority content (articles, guides, frameworks, social)
- DIY paid products/courses
- Cohort-based programmes (group model)
- Quiz-led discovery (`quiz.accelerator-x.ai`)

Purpose:

- Build trust and demand at scale
- Create qualified inbound for higher tiers
- Deliver value without requiring founder 1:1 time

### Layer 2 — Strategic access (selective founder access)

Includes:

- Leadership Activation (entry point)
- Focused strategic cycles for qualified clients
- Selective high-value advisory/coaching access

Purpose:

- Reserve founder time for premium, high-leverage engagements
- Convert qualified buyers into measurable transformation outcomes

### Layer 3 — Implementation (team-led, founder-overseen)

Includes:

- n8n automation and integration delivery
- AI systems/tooling implementation
- Technical programme execution led via delivery partners/team

Purpose:

- Capture implementation demand without founder execution bottlenecks
- Keep founders in architecture/strategy oversight, not production delivery

## 7) Founder involvement policy (scale safeguard)

To avoid repeating capacity constraints, apply these boundaries:

- Layer 1: no mandatory founder delivery dependency
- Layer 2: founder time is selective, premium, and capacity-capped
- Layer 3: founders define direction/quality bars, do not become build operators

Any new offer must specify:

- owner (who delivers)
- founder-time requirement per customer
- scale mode (linear vs non-linear)

If founder-time dependency is high and unavoidable, the offer is not scalable and must be treated as constrained premium inventory.
