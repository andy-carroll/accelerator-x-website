# AX User Needs and Journeys

*Version 1.0 — March 2026*  
*Status: Decision-ready IA and UX input for urgent offer/funnel work*

---

## 1) Purpose

This document defines the user needs that navigation and landing pages must solve now.

Scope:

- user intent states
- top tasks and anxieties
- required wayfinding outcomes
- destination expectations (what users must find after clicking)

This is not a visual design spec. It is a behavioural architecture reference.

---

## 2) Primary user intent states

### Intent A — "I need help now" (workshop-ready)

Typical user:

- senior decision-maker with immediate pressure to move on AI

Needs:

- rapid understanding of what AX does
- confidence this is strategic, not generic AI training
- clear first step and pricing orientation

Success signal:

- user takes action on Leadership Activation path

### Intent B — "I want to join the next cohort" (cohort-intent)

Typical user:

- leader or operator who wants structured group acceleration

Needs:

- clarity on who cohort is for and who it is not for
- timing and commitment expectations
- direct signup/apply mechanism

Success signal:

- user submits cohort form (application/waitlist)

### Intent C — "I am evaluating credibility" (general explorer)

Typical user:

- cautious evaluator comparing options

Needs:

- clear positioning and differentiation
- evidence and method clarity
- low-friction routes to explore without overwhelm

Success signal:

- user progresses to a relevant offer page or quiz

### Intent D — "I came for something specific" (returning user)

Typical user:

- previously engaged visitor with known destination in mind

Needs:

- shortest possible route to target page
- no forced narrative detours

Success signal:

- user reaches destination in <=2 clicks

## 3) Top user jobs to be done (JTBD)

1. Understand what AX does in one pass
2. Self-identify the right next step (workshop, cohort, quiz)
3. Compare commitment levels quickly
4. Take action without needing a call first
5. Trust that follow-up will be timely and relevant

---

## 4) User anxieties to explicitly resolve

Common anxieties to handle in content and IA:

- "Is this strategy-level or just tool demos?"
- "Is this another agency sales funnel?"
- "Is this relevant to my business context?"
- "How much founder access do I actually get?"
- "What does it cost, and what happens next?"
- "Will I be sold into something vague?"

Each primary offer destination should answer these directly.

---

## 5) Navigation requirements derived from user needs

### Wayfinding rules

- Every primary offer destination reachable in 1 click from primary navigation logic.
- High-value routes reachable in <=2 clicks (<=3 edge cases only).
- At least two valid paths to priority offers (by offer type and by user intent).

### Clarity rules

- Labels must use user language, not internal framework jargon.
- Navigation should orient by outcomes and next steps, not catalogue complexity.
- Returning users should never need to re-interpret site structure.

### Priority routes (must exist on every relevant page)

- Leadership Activation route
- Next Cohort route
- Quiz route (`quiz.accelerator-x.ai`)

---

## 6) Destination requirements (what users must find after click)

A click is only successful if destination pages include:

- clear page purpose in headline
- audience fit/non-fit criteria
- expected outcomes
- commitment level (time, effort)
- starting price or pricing model
- one dominant CTA and one secondary CTA maximum
- explicit next-step confirmation after form submission

---

## 7) Conversion and trust requirements

For urgent launch pages (workshop/cohort):

- clear promise and scope boundaries
- no ambiguous CTA labels
- visible social proof/evidence where available
- transparent process for what happens after signup
- response-time expectation (e.g., "we respond within 1 business day")

---

## 8) Measurement gates (v1)

Track these to validate IA and page effectiveness:

- First-click success on top 3 tasks: >=80%
- Task completion to target page in <=2 clicks: >=80%
- Form completion rate on workshop/cohort pages
- Click-through to quiz from homepage/banner/content pages
- Drop-off points between offer page sections and CTA
